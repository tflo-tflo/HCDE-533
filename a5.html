Lamp planning

April 27th, 2024

I took apart one of my table lamps to see how they constructed theirs. It was also helpfuul to help me think through the parts I'll need.

I consulted with Nathan on the direction and feasibility of my lamp ideas. Considerations we discussed:

  1. Lightbulb heat - Possible effects on melting the PLA
  2. Weight - The lamp innerds and cord will naturally shift the weight of a 3D print
  3. Light strength - The brightness will depend on the bulb, the number of walls for the shade, and of the course the design for the light direction

Meshes

April 28th, 2024

The two separate meshes: a horse and a hand.

  1. Slice the horse using a Vertical Plane and Rotate the plane so that it would protude slightly upwards (use the front view and Rotate, *not* Rotate 3D)
  2. Scale both meshes so the horse's head is roughly half the size of the palm
  3. Rotate the horse so it's facing the same direction as the open palm
  4. MeshBooleanUnion
  5. ReduceMesh by 90%
  6. Turn on "Flat Shading" in the Display setting for the Shaded display mode, in Perspective
  7. MeshRepair - I initially had a bad mesh, repaired it, then was left with unused vertices
  8. RebuildMesh - This resolved the unused vertices
  9. MeshRepair
  10. Check in slicer program
