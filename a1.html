Asssignment 1
Modelling and laser cutting: 30 instances of one unique part

Make a kit consisting of at least 30 instances of at least one unique part! The parts must have slots that press fit into the other parts in the kit! The parts must be laser cut out of cardboard! Your fit should be such that the parts do not come apart with a light shaking!

  • Source files
  • Laser cutting settings: 25% speed, 100% power, vector

Ideation

March 30th, 2024

With a little inspiration from Kenya Hara's "Architecture for Dogs" I decided to make something that my friend's cats could use, a cone cat scratcher.

After sketching a few options, I consulted a friend who's built models using laser cutting on the scope and difficulty of each idea. We agreed idea B seemed doable while still a challenge.

Half-scaled prototype

March 30th, 2024

Measurements. The final product should be 16" tall, the cats when standing measure at 14".

I didn't have a compass for the circles. Since they weren't perfectly cut, I marked 8 diameters to position the slats. Below is a prototype with a quarter of the slats I'm thinking of producing.

Prepping materials

March 30th, 2024

Found moving boxes in the recyling bin and getting them all cleaned up and prepped. Cutting out one slat to scale with the actual material to check measurements and ensure I have enough supplies to work with. This cardboard thickness measures at around 3/16".

Cuttle model

March 31st, 2024

Adjust model to account for material removed by the laser by taking the cardboard thickeness minus 1/16" and adding chamfers.

Cut and build

March 31st, 2024

Doing larger pieces resulted in a few hitches. Not sure if it's due to the size, but the laser couldn't cut through fully by the time it reached the third slat on the print bed. Or that the slots for fitting would be more difficult to fit together when they were longer.

Shake test and retrospective

March 31st, 2024

For starters, I think I got the assignment wrong! It should have been a modular kit that you could build a variation of structures with. If I were to do it again, I would work at a much smaller scale. I learnt a few things about working larger sizes but ended up spending more time preppping the material.
